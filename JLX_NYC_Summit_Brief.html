The Survey and the Report

JLX launched the Future of Learning Professionals Industry Skills Survey to answer one question:

What skills and professional development do learning professionals believe they need within the next three years to remain effective as AI reshapes design, delivery, and strategy?

Wave 1 closed April 19 with 133 responses (n=133). Respondents assessed nine skill areas and reflected on the gap between their own priorities and their employer's. The survey reached learning professionals across higher education, K-12, corporate, government, nonprofit, consulting, and association sectors. Wave 2 is open now through May 11.

From Survey to Summit

The survey, report, and summit are one connected initiative:

Apr 19 Wave 1 closed. 133 responses collected across sectors.
Apr 24 Wave 1 pre-report. First findings published and distributed.
May 11 Full survey closes. Final data collection across all sectors.
May 15 Full industry report. Complete findings published and distributed to all participants.
Jun 18 NYC Summit. Attendees arrive having read the report. The day is spent working with the data -- pressure-testing findings, surfacing disagreements, and building a skills roadmap together.

The report does not end the conversation. The summit is where the profession does something with it.

The Summit

An application-based gathering of up to 100 learning professionals, 9 AM to 4 PM, with an optional informal dinner after. Two key audiences: senior thought leaders and practitioners presenting and participating, and younger emerging professionals including graduate students. The application process will balance both groups across sector, background, and career stage.

Morning
Four to five curated talks selected through a competitive pitch process, with structured table discussion between sessions.
Midday
Assigned lunch -- attendees connect with someone outside their usual context. Cross-pollination, not networking.
Afternoon
Working session. Small groups use the report data to build a draft skills roadmap -- a tangible output that feeds the 2027 national conference.

This is a Northeast regional event. The model is local and repeatable.

Nine skill areas under the lens:
  • Learning strategy & design thinking
  • Content development
  • Facilitation and delivery
  • Learning technology
  • AI tools and automation
  • Data analysis and reporting
  • Storytelling and narrative design
  • Stakeholder influence & strategic communication
  • Change management & org development
