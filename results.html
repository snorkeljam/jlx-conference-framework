AI may be the first force in a generation that genuinely levels the playing field across sectors. We ran this survey because we believe corporate learning, higher education, K-12, consulting, and workforce development should be figuring this out together — and that practitioners, not vendors, should be driving the conversation.

In Spring of 2026 we ran a survey asking learning professionals about what skills will matter over the next three years and what barriers limit their development. The survey did not ask about the state of the profession but many used the open text to talk about it anyway. Here are some of the responses, categorized.

01 — Skill Priorities

Strategy beats AI.

When asked to rate nine skills on a five-point scale, learning strategy and design thinking ranked first — not AI. AI tools came in third, behind stakeholder influence. The profession's ambient anxiety is pointed at AI. Its considered judgment is pointed somewhere else.

02 — The Perception Gap

What practitioners value vs. what employers see.

Respondents rated each skill twice: once for their own priorities, once for what they believed their employer valued. Storytelling produced the largest gap in the dataset. Data analysis ran the other direction — the only skill where employer expectations exceeded practitioner priorities.
