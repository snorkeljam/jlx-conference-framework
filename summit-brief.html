01 — The Idea

Nobody has the full answer, yet.

AI may be the first force in a generation that genuinely levels the playing field across sectors. Across corporate learning, higher education, K-12, consulting, association management, and workforce development, we are all trying to figure out our future.

Nobody has the full answer, yet. That's why we think we should do this together.

The JLX Convening brings senior learning leaders and emerging learning professionals together, across sectors, to do something the field rarely does: sit in the same room and work on shared problems.

The output is a practitioner-built skills roadmap. Not a vendor presentation. Not a keynote. A working document that reflects what the profession actually needs, built by the people who do this work every day.

02 — Possible Structure

One day, built for working.

An application-based gathering, designed to balance sector representation, background, and career stage. Running 9 AM to 4 PM, with an optional informal dinner after.

Morning
Four to five curated talks selected through a competitive pitch process, with structured table discussion between sessions.
Midday
Assigned lunch — attendees connect with someone outside their usual context. Cross-pollination, not networking.
Afternoon
Working session. Small groups build a draft skills roadmap together — a tangible output that feeds future work across the profession.
Evening
Optional dinner for those who want to continue the conversation.
