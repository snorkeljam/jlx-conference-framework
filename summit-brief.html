JLX Convening
Cross-Sector Learning Professionals

A Future Skills Summit for Learning Professionals
Organized by JLX

The Idea

AI may be the first force in a generation that genuinely levels the playing field across sectors. Across corporate learning, higher education, K-12, consulting, association management, and workforce development, we are all trying to figure out our future.

Nobody has the full answer, yet. That's why we think we should do this together.

The JLX Convening brings senior learning leaders and emerging learning professionals together — across sectors — to do something the field rarely does: sit in the same room and work on shared problems.

The output is a practitioner-built skills roadmap. Not a vendor presentation. Not a keynote. A working document that reflects what the profession actually needs, built by the people who do this work every day.

Possible Structure

An application-based gathering, designed to balance sector representation, background, and career stage. Running 9 AM to 4 PM, with an optional informal dinner after.

Morning
Four to five curated talks selected through a competitive pitch process, with structured table discussion between sessions.
Midday
Assigned lunch — attendees connect with someone outside their usual context. Cross-pollination, not networking.
Afternoon
Working session. Small groups build a draft skills roadmap together — a tangible output that feeds future work across the profession.
Evening
Optional dinner for those who want to continue the conversation.
Nine skill areas under the lens:
  • Learning strategy & design thinking
  • Content development
  • Facilitation and delivery
  • Learning technology
  • AI tools and automation
  • Data analysis and reporting
  • Storytelling and narrative design
  • Stakeholder influence & strategic communication
  • Change management & org development

Who We Are Building This With

We are seeking one or two community partners per sector — organizations and leaders who want to help shape this, not just attend it. Technology vendors are welcome as sponsors.

Corporate Learning Workplace learning teams and talent development functions inside organizations.
Higher Education Universities, academic programs, and faculty development organizations.
K-12 Teachers, instructional coaches, and curriculum designers in schools and districts.
Consulting Freelance instructional designers, learning consultants, and independent practitioners.
Association Mgmt Professional associations running conferences, certifications, and member education programs.
Workforce Development Government, nonprofit, and community-based organizations focused on workforce and skills.

Get Involved

This event is in development. We are actively looking for:

  • Community partners in each sector
  • Speakers and practitioners with something to say
  • Organizations interested in sponsoring
  • Learning professionals who want to attend

If you are interested in partnering, speaking, sponsoring, or attending, or if you just want to follow the project, reach out directly. And if you do not see your sector represented here, tell us what is missing.
